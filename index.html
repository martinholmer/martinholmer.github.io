Background

I have a Ph.D. in economics from MIT and decades of experience designing, developing and using microsimulation models that estimate the effects of changes in public policy.

Since 2015, I have been the main developer of Tax-Calculator, an open-source static federal income and payroll tax microsimulation model written in Python. In 2018, the World Bank asked us to work with the Tax Policy Research Unit of the Ministry of Finance in New Delhi to adapt Tax-Calculator to simulate the Indian personal income tax, goods and services tax, and corporate income tax. That project is maintained in this TPRU-India repository.

Over several decades before 2016, I designed and developed, for use by federal government agencies and non-profit organizations, a suite of dynamic microsimulation models — known as the PSG Models — that simulate payroll taxes and social security benefits as well the lifetime accumulation of employer-sponsored pension benefits. The models are written in C++ and have a graphical user interface that facilitates their use.
